# Method Rules: Tracking Institutional Big Money Flow in Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX)

## 1. Primary Method: Net Foreign Buy/Sell Analysis

The most accessible proxy for institutional flow in Indonesia is monitoring **Net Foreign (NF) Buy and Sell** data.

### Key Definitions
- **Net Foreign Buy (NFB):** Foreign investors buy more than they sell → Bullish signal, capital inflow
- **Net Foreign Sell (NFS):** Foreign investors sell more than they buy → Bearish signal, capital outflow

### Tracking Rules
| Timeframe | Action | Purpose |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Daily** | Check daily net foreign value | Gauge immediate institutional sentiment |
| **Weekly** | Track accumulated weekly net flow | Identify short-term trends |
| **Monthly/YTD** | Monitor accumulated monthly net flow | Confirm long-term institutional trend direction |

---

## 2. Stock Selection Rules: The "Top List" Method

Institutions concentrate their activity in specific stocks. Monitor where the volume is concentrated.

### Actionable Rules
| Rule | Description | Target Stocks |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **1. Focus on Top Lists** | Stocks appearing consistently on Top Foreign Buy lists indicate institutional accumulation | INCO, ASII, BBRI, BBCA (common institutional targets) |
| **2. Follow Liquidity** | Institutions need high liquidity for entry/exit → focus on Big Caps | LQ45 constituents, Kompas100 index members |
| **3. Watch Sector Rotation** | Track shifts between sectors (e.g., Banking → Commodities) | Compare sector representation in daily top lists |

## 4. Advanced Interpretation Rules

### Rule A: Herding Behavior Recognition
- **Observation:** Both domestic and foreign institutions on IDX exhibit herding behavior (buying/selling in groups)
- **Application:** Sudden coordinated moves in top lists signal trend initiation or exhaustion

### Rule B: Domestic Institution Tracking
- **Research finding:** Domestic institutional investors are often the most influential variable in market movement
- **Application:** Monitor mutual funds and asset manager activity when data is available

### Rule C: Weight-Based Analysis
- **Current IDX Structure (2025):**
  - Retail investors: ~50% of transactions (dominant by count)
  - Foreign institutions: ~36% of transaction value (dominant by value)
- **Application:** Follow the money value, not the transaction count

---

## 5. Implementation Protocol

### Daily Tracking Routine
1. **Step 1:** Record daily Net Foreign Buy/Sell value
2. **Step 2:** Log Top 5 stocks by foreign buy
3. **Step 3:** Log Top 5 stocks by foreign sell
4. **Step 4:** Note any sector concentration patterns

### Weekly Analysis
- Calculate accumulated net flow
- Identify persistent institutional favorites (stocks appearing 3+ days)
- Compare with LQ45 index movement

### Monthly Review
- Analyze sector rotation trends
- Compare with IHSG index performance
- Adjust watchlist based on accumulated data patterns

---

## 6. Critical Limitations

| Limitation | Mitigation Strategy |
|:---|:---|
| Foreign flow ≠ All institutional flow | Cross-reference with domestic institutional data when available |
| Daily volatility creates noise | Use weekly/monthly accumulated data for confirmation |
| Institutions also make wrong decisions | Combine flow analysis with fundamentals and technicals |
| Delayed data reporting | Focus on trends rather than real-time reaction |

---

**Core Principle:** Track the whales (institutional value), follow the accumulation (consistent buying), and respect the liquidity (LQ45 focus).
